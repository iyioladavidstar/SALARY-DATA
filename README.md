‚ùì Research Questions and Interpretation
1. What is the average, minimum, and maximum salary of employees?

Interpretation:
Using Pivot Tables, salary summary statistics provide a general understanding of income distribution. The average salary represents the typical earning level, while the minimum and maximum salaries indicate income inequality within the organization.

Conclusion:
Employee salaries vary significantly, suggesting differences in experience, education, and job roles contribute to income disparities.

2. How does years of experience affect salary levels?

Interpretation:
Pivot Table analysis shows that employees with more years of experience generally earn higher salaries. Salary tends to increase progressively as experience increases.

Conclusion:
There is a positive relationship between years of experience and salary. More experienced employees are compensated with higher pay.

3. Is there a significant difference in salary between male and female employees?

Interpretation:
Comparing average salaries by gender using Pivot Tables reveals whether one gender earns more on average than the other.

Conclusion:
Any observed salary difference may indicate gender-based pay variation, though further statistical testing would be required to confirm significance.

4. What is the effect of education level on employee salary?

Interpretation:
Employees with higher educational qualifications (Master‚Äôs and PhD) show higher average salaries compared to those with Bachelor‚Äôs degrees.

Conclusion:
Education level has a positive effect on salary. Higher academic qualifications are associated with higher earnings.

5. How does job title influence salary variation?

Interpretation:
Salary analysis by job title shows that senior or specialized roles earn significantly more than entry-level positions.

Conclusion:
Job title is a strong determinant of salary, reflecting responsibility level and skill requirements.

6. Does age significantly influence salary after controlling for experience and education?

Interpretation:
When age is considered alongside experience and education, its direct effect on salary appears weaker than experience.

Conclusion:
Age alone does not strongly determine salary; experience and education play more critical roles.
